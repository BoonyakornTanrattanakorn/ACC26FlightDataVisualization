Flight phases & scoring

Each scored flight is measured automatically and follows three phases (rulebook §4.6). The flight-time start t₀ is the first moment the motor current exceeds 5 A for more than 3 s, or GPS ground speed reaches 5 km/h — whichever comes first.

Climb
t₀ → t₀+60 s · altitude not scored
Distance
t₀+60 → t₀+180 s · scored window
Landing
after t₀+180 s · must be safe

How a round score is built (rulebook §4.7)

  • Raw score = Btake-off · mpayload · l²distance · (1 − Pcurrent), where ldistance is the ground-projected distance flown in the 120 s distance segment, and the take-off bonus B is 1.15 for a 40 m declared run, 1 for 60 m, and 0 if the aircraft cannot take off within its declared length.
  • Normalized to the best raw score in the round × 1000, then + loading/unloading bonus (up to 60), + payload-prediction bonus (up to 60), − flight penalties. Max 1120 per flight.
  • Flight-competition score = mean of a team's two best rounds (or its single best round if fewer than three were flown).
  • Total competition score = flight competition + report (≤200) + drawings (≤50) + presentation (≤100) − global penalties. Max 1470.

The normalized score depends on the announced take-off length, loading/unloading times and payload prediction, which are not in the telemetry — so this dashboard shows the official round scores and derives only the physical metrics (distance, speeds, phases) from the flight logs.
