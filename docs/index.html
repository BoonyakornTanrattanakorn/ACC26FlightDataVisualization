The Air Cargo Challenge

The Air Cargo Challenge (ACC) is a biennial aeronautical engineering competition that brings together university students from across Europe to design, build and fly a radio-controlled cargo aircraft. First held in Lisbon in 2003 — in the spirit of the North American Design/Build/Fly competition — it has grown into one of the continent's largest student aerospace events.

The 2026 edition is hosted by Akamodell Stuttgart, the student aeromodelling club of the University of Stuttgart and winners of ACC 2024, with coordination by EUROAVIA. It runs 30 June – 3 July 2026, with technical presentations at the University of Stuttgart (Vaihingen campus) and the flight competition at the MFV Böblingen airfield.

The flight mission

The task of ACC 2026 is to transport as many 0.33 l soda cans as possible, as fast as possible. Each scored flight is measured automatically and follows the same profile:

A take-off with 60 s of climb is followed by a 120 s distance segment and a safe landing. All teams fly the same prescribed T-MOTOR powertrain, so results reflect the airframe rather than pilot skill. Bonus points reward a short runway and fast loading/unloading; exceeding the 30 A current limit incurs penalties. A team's competition score combines its best flight rounds with marks for the technical presentation, drawings and report, minus penalties.
